High CPU Usage:
High CPU usage is often caused by runaway processes, infinite loops, or resource-intensive applications.
Use 'top' or 'htop' to identify the process consuming the most CPU.
Check application logs for errors or infinite loops.
Use 'kill -9 PID' to terminate the runaway process.
Always perform RCA after resolving to prevent recurrence.

Memory Leaks:
Memory leaks occur when applications allocate memory but fail to release it.
Symptoms include gradually increasing memory usage over time.
Use 'free -m' to check available memory.
Use 'ps aux --sort=-%mem' to find memory-hungry processes.
Restart the affected service as immediate mitigation.

Incident Response:
First step in incident response is to acknowledge the alert immediately.
Identify the blast radius — how many users or systems are affected.
Communicate status updates every 15 minutes to stakeholders.
Mitigate first, root cause later.
Document everything in the incident ticket in real time.

Monitoring:
The four golden signals are latency, traffic, errors, and saturation.
Latency measures how long a request takes to respond.
Traffic measures the demand on your system.
Errors measure the rate of failed requests.
Saturation measures how full your system is.
